My Top 3 Favourite Movies

This is my inter-changeable list of my Favourite movies, i have always thought of my favourite movies as movies i can watch multiple times without getting bored, and i feel like i did a pretty good job of shortening that list into just 3 pick's!

Table of Contents:

  1. Whiplash

    2. Whiplash is a 2014 American psychological drama film written and directed by Damien Chazelle, and starring Miles Teller, J. K. Simmons, and Paul Reiser. It focuses on an ambitious music student and aspiring jazz drummer (Teller), who is pushed to his limit by his abusive instructor (Simmons).

  2. Perfect Days

    3. Perfect Days is a 2023 drama film directed by Wim Wenders from a script written by Wenders and Takuma Takasaki. A co-production between Japan and Germany, the film follows the routine life of Hirayama (Kōji Yakusho), a public toilet cleaner in Tokyo.

  3. Memories of Murder

    4. Memories of Murder (Korean: 살인의 추억) is a 2003 South Korean neo-noir crime thriller film directed by Bong Joon Ho, from a screenplay by Bong and Shim Sung-bo, and based on the 1996 play Come to See Me by Kim Kwang-lim. It stars Song Kang-ho and Kim Sang-kyung. In the film, detectives Park Doo-man (Song) and Seo Tae-yoon (Kim) lead an investigation into a string of rapes and murders taking place in Hwaseong in the late 1980s. The film is based on the real life killings carried out by Lee Choon-jae.

Whiplash (2014)

This might be my favourite movie of all time because of how great the story is, i love the acting in this! JK Simmons absolutely killed this role like he was born for it, and the emotions that Miles Teller's character went through is just like a tragic hero story, and it really gets me into the story.

Perfect Days (2023)

I watched this movie early in 2025 when i was just getting into watching movies, and it didn't really hit me as much at first, i thought this was just gonna be some generic slice of life movie with not much going for it. Boy was i wrong! this movie had some of the most amazing camera sequences i have ever seen, the color grading is stunning, the changes in music really emphasized the raw emotions the main character feels every scene, the silent waves when he is dreaming, the sound of leaves and toilet cleaning in the park, this is just a movie i can watch for the rest of my life and i won't get bored of it.

Check out the IMDB page for this movie!

Memories Of Murder

I think this is my favourite korean movie of all time, Bong Joon Ho is truly a master of his work, even though this was one of his earlier works, you see some of the techniques that he still uses in his newer movies (e.g Parasite(2019)) and i really like the atmospheric eerie feeling that you feel everytime you watch, the deafening silence and crazy plot-twists, really reminded me of another detective movie called Cure(1997).
